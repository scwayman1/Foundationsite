A simpler path

From “Is this accessible?” to “Here’s what to do next.”

The proposed service handles technical machinery in the background and brings faculty into the loop only where human judgment genuinely matters.

01 / INTAKE

Bring the document

PDFs, Word files, or PowerPoints would enter a secure, policy-controlled workflow—without faculty-side specialist software.

02 / ANALYZE

Find the barriers

Standards-based validators would inspect structure, tags, images, tables, links, language, and more.

03 / REVIEW

Fix with guidance

Low-risk repairs could be proposed while faculty review alt text and other meaning-dependent changes.

04 / RELEASE

Download with evidence

The future workflow would preserve a revised document, remaining exceptions, and a clear processing record.

What it would check

Serious standards. Human language.

The proposed platform separates machine-verifiable rules from AI-assisted suggestions and human review. That distinction prevents useful automation from becoming compliance theater.

↳

Document structure

Tags, headings, lists, title, language, bookmarks, and logical organization.

◫

Images and charts

Missing descriptions, decorative images, and draft alternative text for faculty review.

▦

Tables

Headers, associations, complexity, and warnings when specialist review is the honest answer.

≡

Reading order

A screen-reader-oriented sequence for confirming that a document makes sense out loud.

Aa

Links and language

Descriptive links, declared language, readable labels, and navigable document controls.

✓

Validation record

What passed, what was repaired, what was reviewed, and what still requires an exception.

Institutional visibility

Support faculty without turning accessibility into surveillance.

Leaders could see progress, risk, and support demand by department. Document contents would remain private by default; the dashboard measures the process, not the professor.

  • Track documents checked and ready to publish
  • Identify departments needing training or specialist help
  • Measure turnaround and recurring accessibility barriers
  • Preserve an auditable record of systematic effort
