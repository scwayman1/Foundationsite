# Coastline Alumni Segmentation Framework

This document outlines the segmentation framework for the Coastline College Alumni Association. This framework is designed to be used within the GradRoots CRM and action layer to enable personalized, proactive, and value-driven engagement with Coastline alumni.

## 1. Workforce Alumni

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Workforce alumni are the most direct evidence of Coastline's economic impact. They are employed in local and regional industries, and their success is a testament to the value of a Coastline education. They are also a key source of employer partnerships, internships, and career opportunities for current students. |
| **Common Signals** | LinkedIn profile updates, company news, industry publications, participation in professional organizations, and self-reported employment data. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Industry-specific newsletters, invitations to networking events, and requests for mentorship or guest speaking opportunities. |
| **Value Pathways** | Employer partnerships, internships, apprenticeships, curriculum feedback, and industry-specific financial support. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would your company be interested in partnering with Coastline to create a new internship program?" or "Would you be willing to speak to a group of current students about your career path?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Professional, respectful, and focused on mutual value. |

## 2. Transfer Alumni

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Transfer alumni are a testament to Coastline's role as a launchpad for further academic achievement. Their success at four-year institutions validates the quality of a Coastline education and provides aspirational pathways for current students. |
| **Common Signals** | National Student Clearinghouse data, LinkedIn profile updates showing a new degree, and self-reported educational attainment. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | University-specific alumni events, transfer-focused workshops, and requests for mentorship of current transfer students. |
| **Value Pathways** | Joint alumni events with four-year institutions, transfer scholarships, and testimonials for marketing materials. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be willing to share your transfer experience with current Coastline students?" or "Would you be interested in supporting a scholarship for transfer students?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Encouraging, supportive, and focused on academic achievement. |

## 3. Adult Re-entry Alumni

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Adult re-entry alumni demonstrate Coastline's commitment to lifelong learning and career transformation. They often have significant life and professional experience, making them valuable mentors and advocates. |
| **Common Signals** | Enrollment in continuing education courses, career changes later in life, and participation in alumni events focused on professional development. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Flexible and online engagement opportunities, professional development workshops, and networking events with other adult learners. |
| **Value Pathways** | Mentorship, guest speaking, and financial support for programs that serve adult learners. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be interested in mentoring a current adult re-entry student?" or "Would you be willing to share your story of career change to inspire others?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Empathetic, respectful, and focused on personal and professional growth. |

## 4. Military-Connected Alumni

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Military-connected alumni are a key constituency for Coastline, reflecting the college's long-standing commitment to serving the armed forces. They bring unique skills, experiences, and leadership qualities to the alumni community. |
| **Common Signals** | Self-reported military service, participation in veterans' groups, and engagement with Coastline's military and veterans services. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Military-specific networking events, partnerships with veteran service organizations, and targeted support for military-to-civilian career transitions. |
| **Value Pathways** | Mentorship of student veterans, advocacy for military-friendly policies, and financial support for veterans' services. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be willing to mentor a student veteran who is transitioning to a civilian career?" or "Would you be interested in supporting a scholarship for military-connected students?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Respectful, appreciative, and focused on service and community. |

## 5. Career Technical Education (CTE) Alumni

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | CTE alumni are a direct link to high-demand industries and a key driver of regional economic development. Their success showcases the value of hands-on, skills-based education. |
| **Common Signals** | Industry certifications, apprenticeships, and employment in skilled trades and technical fields. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Industry-specific advisory boards, hands-on workshops, and partnerships with local employers. |
| **Value Pathways** | Curriculum development, equipment donations, and apprenticeships for current CTE students. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be interested in serving on our advisory board for the automotive technology program?" or "Would your company be willing to donate a piece of equipment to our welding lab?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Practical, results-oriented, and focused on skills and craftsmanship. |

## 6. Geographically Clustered Alumni

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Geographically clustered alumni represent opportunities for localized engagement and community building. They can serve as brand ambassadors and help to strengthen Coastline's presence in key regions. |
| **Common Signals** | Address data, event attendance, and social media location check-ins. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Regional alumni chapters, local networking events, and community service projects. |
| **Value Pathways** | Event hosting, volunteer leadership, and local corporate partnerships. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be interested in helping to organize a Coastline alumni event in your city?" or "Would you be willing to serve as a social media ambassador for your region?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Friendly, community-oriented, and focused on local connections. |

## 7. Employer-Connected Alumni

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Employer-connected alumni are a direct link to companies and organizations that can provide jobs, internships, and other opportunities for Coastline students and graduates. |
| **Common Signals** | Employment at a key local or regional employer, participation in corporate alumni groups, and referrals of current students for job openings. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Corporate alumni events, on-site information sessions, and partnerships with employee resource groups. |
| **Value Pathways** | Job and internship pipelines, corporate sponsorships, and employee giving programs. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would your company be interested in participating in our virtual career fair?" or "Would you be willing to champion a corporate sponsorship for our annual scholarship dinner?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Professional, strategic, and focused on corporate partnerships. |

## 8. Emerging Donors

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Emerging donors are the future of philanthropy at Coastline. Cultivating these relationships early and often is essential for long-term financial sustainability. |
| **Common Signals** | Consistent but small-dollar giving, event attendance, and engagement with fundraising appeals. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Giving societies, behind-the-scenes tours, and personalized stewardship reports. |
| **Value Pathways** | Annual giving, planned giving, and major gifts over time. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be interested in joining our new giving society for donors who make a monthly gift?" or "Would you like to learn more about how you can include Coastline in your estate plans?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Appreciative, inspiring, and focused on impact. |

## 9. Active Volunteers

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Active volunteers are the lifeblood of the alumni association, providing essential support for events, programs, and initiatives. They are also some of Coastline's most passionate advocates. |
| **Common Signals** | Consistent volunteer activity, leadership roles in alumni groups, and recruitment of other volunteers. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Volunteer appreciation events, leadership development opportunities, and increased responsibility. |
| **Value Pathways** | Board membership, committee leadership, and philanthropic support. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be interested in taking on a leadership role in our alumni association?" or "Would you be willing to make a gift to support our volunteer program?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Grateful, empowering, and focused on leadership and service. |

## 10. High Mentorship Potential

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Alumni with high mentorship potential are a valuable resource for current students, providing guidance, support, and professional connections. |
| **Common Signals** | Senior-level job titles, extensive professional experience, and a willingness to give back. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Structured mentorship programs, one-on-one matching with students, and recognition for their service. |
| **Value Pathways** | Student success, workforce development, and a stronger alumni network. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be interested in serving as a mentor to a current Coastline student?" or "Would you be willing to participate in a panel discussion on career development?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Respectful, aspirational, and focused on giving back to the next generation. |

## 11. Lapsed but Reactivatable Alumni

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Lapsed but reactivatable alumni represent a significant opportunity for growth. With the right engagement strategy, they can be brought back into the fold and become active and engaged members of the alumni community. |
| **Common Signals** | Past engagement that has since dropped off, out-of-date contact information, and a lack of recent communication. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Re-engagement campaigns, personalized outreach, and invitations to high-value events. |
| **Value Pathways** | Renewed engagement, volunteerism, and philanthropic support. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "We miss you! Would you be interested in attending our upcoming alumni mixer?" or "We've updated our alumni benefits. Would you like to learn more?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Welcoming, inclusive, and focused on reconnecting. |

## 12. Community Champions

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Community champions are influential alumni who are well-connected in their local communities. They can serve as powerful advocates for Coastline and help to build bridges between the college and the community. |
| **Common Signals** | Leadership roles in community organizations, public service, and a strong local network. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Community advisory boards, public speaking opportunities, and recognition for their community service. |
| **Value Pathways** | Community partnerships, advocacy, and increased visibility for the college. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be interested in serving on our new community advisory board?" or "Would you be willing to speak at a local community event on behalf of Coastline?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Collaborative, civic-minded, and focused on community impact. |

## 13. Alumni with Public Leadership/Board Visibility

| Profile Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Why it Matters** | Alumni with public leadership or board visibility are influential leaders who can open doors and create opportunities for Coastline. Their endorsement can lend credibility and prestige to the college. |
| **Common Signals** | Service on corporate or non-profit boards, elected office, and media mentions. |
| **Engagement Approaches** | Peer-to-peer outreach from college leadership, invitations to exclusive events, and strategic advisory roles. |
| **Value Pathways** | High-level partnerships, major gifts, and transformational opportunities for the college. |
| **Appropriate Asks** | "Would you be interested in joining the Coastline College Foundation Board of Directors?" or "Would you be willing to host a private dinner for a select group of alumni and community leaders?" |
| **Messaging Tone** | Professional, strategic, and focused on high-impact leadership. |
